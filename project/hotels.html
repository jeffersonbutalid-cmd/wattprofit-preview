SEAI plus ACA, modelled together

Two reliefs, one cash flow. Most installer quotes only show one.

Hotels qualify for SEAI Non-Domestic Microgen (under 50 kWp) or SEAI Commercial Solar (typically 6 kWp to larger commercial scales), plus the Accelerated Capital Allowance against corporation tax on qualifying Triple-E-registered equipment, written off 100% in year one.

For a profitable owner-operated hotel paying corporation tax at 12.5%, the ACA can be worth €15,000 to €30,000 in year one cash flow on a typical 50 to 100 kWp system. That moves payback by 6 to 14 months.

The battery question

Hotels have meaningful evening load. Batteries change the maths.

A dairy farm rarely justifies a battery. A hotel often does. Your evening dinner service, your laundry cycle, and your second HVAC ramp all sit after the PV has gone to bed. The question is whether the round-trip economics beat the SEM export price.

We model the no-battery scenario, a 30 kWh battery, and a 60 kWh battery side by side. Roughly a third of the hotels we see justify the storage. The rest do not, and we tell them so.

Hotel questions, answered

The four questions hotel owners ask us first.

My accountant has never claimed ACA. Is it real? +

It is. ACA (Accelerated Capital Allowance) is administered by Revenue against equipment listed on the SEAI Triple-E Product Register. We flag in the report which inverter and panel makes on your installer's quote are eligible and which are not. Most hotel accountants we work with are happy once they see the supporting documentation.

We're loss-making this year. Can we still take ACA? +

The unused allowance carries forward indefinitely against future trading profits, so the cash benefit lands the year you next make a taxable profit. We model both cases (relief in year 1 vs. relief deferred to year 3) so you see both payback paths.

Our installer says we need a battery. Do we? +

Maybe. For a typical 60-room hotel without a leisure centre, we usually find batteries lengthen payback by 9 to 18 months even though they raise NPV. For a 120-room hotel with a pool plant cycling overnight, the picture often reverses. We always show you both scenarios side by side and explain which assumption flips the answer.

Will solar affect our Green Hospitality grading? +

Yes, positively. Renewable on-site generation contributes to the energy criteria for Gold and Platinum awards under Green Hospitality, and the data we provide (kWh generated, kWh self-consumed, CO₂ avoided against the all-island grid factor) is in the format their assessors look for. We can liaise directly with your assessor on request.
