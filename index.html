How it works

Three steps. Twenty-four hours.

No site visit needed at this stage. The half-hourly data tells us everything we need to size four candidate systems against your actual load and against current SEM export rates.

Start the 24-hour clock
  1. 01

    Send half-hourly data.

    Email your ESB Networks HDF file, or forward us your last electricity bill and we'll request the data on your behalf. Two minutes of your time. You'll need your MPRN.

  2. 02

    We model four system sizes.

    Against your real load, on your roof or yard orientation, with SEAI or TAMS or ACA priced in correctly, with battery and no-battery scenarios where the load justifies it.

  3. 03

    One-page report with the exact payback year.

    PDF, in 24 hours, signed off by a chartered engineer. Take it to your installer, your accountant, or your bank. We've no skin in which way you go next.

Common questions

The four questions every prospect asks us.

Why independent? What's the catch? +

No catch. We charge a fixed fee (€395 in most cases). We take no commission from installers, no rebate, no kickback. We're paid to give you a number you can act on, not to push hardware. We send you a copy of our installer-conflicts policy on request.

How do you handle SEAI vs TAMS vs ACA? +

Dairy farms apply for TAMS 3 (up to 60% of eligible spend, ceiling depends on your TAMS investment ceiling). Hotels apply for SEAI Non-Domestic Microgen or SEAI Commercial Solar, plus 100% first-year ACA against corporation tax on qualifying spend. We model only the grant lines you actually qualify for, and we cap system size at the grant ceiling unless oversizing has a clear NPV case.

What if our installer disagrees with your number? +

It happens. Most often it's because the installer assumed 100% self-consumption, or assumed a higher export tariff than the SEM is currently paying. We'll get on a call with you and the installer, walk through the workings, and update either side's number if the assumption was wrong. We've revised our own number in roughly 1 in 10 cases. We've revised the installer's in roughly 6 in 10.

Do you visit the site? +

Not at the modelling stage, and that's deliberate. The half-hourly meter data and roof imagery are sufficient to give you a payback year you can decide on. If your number looks viable and you want a structural survey before committing capex, we'll refer you to two independent surveyors in your county. No commission either way.

An installer's quote is a starting price. An independent payback year is a decision.
